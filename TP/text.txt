A soft breeze carried distant laughter over the alleyway.
The old clock chimed five, but no one rushed to answer.
Paper boats lined the gutter, each holding a tiny folded wish.
Lantern light pooled on the cobblestones like warm honey.
She traced the map with a fingertip and smiled at the absurd route.
An unfinished song hummed from the neighbor's open window.
Rain left silver fingerprints on the iron railing.
He kept a stack of stamped envelopes for letters he never sent.
Midnight smelled of jasmine and the memory of summer.
Footsteps echoed as if the street were remembering its past.
The horizon promised a new day, painted in uncertain blue.
